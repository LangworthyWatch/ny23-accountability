# Facebook post: Essential Plan cliff (July 1, 2026)
Companion to essential_plan_cliff_card.png and the fact-check.

---

Today, about 450,000 New Yorkers lose the Essential Plan, the free health coverage they had through the state. That is the New York State Department of Health's own count.

Here is what the headline leaves out: who loses it, and whether they can afford what is left.

→ Who: working people earning roughly $31,300 to $40,000 a year (200 to 250 percent of the poverty line), a large share of them lawfully present immigrants.

→ What replaces the free plan: a marketplace plan that costs as much as $250 a month plus a $2,500 deductible, even after tax credits. That is about 10 percent of a single person's income in premiums alone, before the deductible.

→ Will they take it up? Before the Essential Plan covered this group, only about 78,000 people in this income range bought a marketplace plan, roughly 17 percent. Analysts now project as many as 80 percent may go uninsured rather than buy in.

Where did the money go? New York's health department names the cause directly: H.R. 1, the federal budget law. The Congressional Budget Office scored the whole law: over 2026 to 2034 it cuts about $900 billion from Medicaid and SNAP while adding $3.3 trillion to household resources through tax cuts, concentrated at the top.

The NY-23 record: Rep. Langworthy voted Aye on H.R. 1 (House Roll Call 190, July 2025). The Fiscal Policy Institute estimates about 26,000 people in Western New York lose Essential Plan coverage.

You can support work requirements and still think handing a $3,000-a-year bill to people earning $31,000 goes further than most folks realize.
