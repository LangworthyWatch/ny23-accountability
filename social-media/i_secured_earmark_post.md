When Nick Langworthy posts "I secured" federal dollars for the district, what does that actually mean? We built the ledger, cross-referencing his House earmarks against the Senate's.

→ About half of his roughly $32M in earmarks (about $17.7M) is on projects Senators Schumer and Gillibrand fund too. All three offices announce the same dollars as their own.

→ On the biggest shared projects, the senators asked FIRST. Langworthy took office in 2023, so his first earmark cycle was 2024. Gillibrand had already requested West Seneca ($7.4M), Portville ($5.25M), and Mayville ($2M) back in 2022, before he could file anything.

→ Some of what he claims isn't even in his district: the West Seneca water project and the Buffalo airport (about $2.7M combined) are in NY-26, Tim Kennedy's district.

To be fair: about half his earmarks (about $14.9M) are genuinely his own work, led by $4.2M for Erie County Sheriff helicopters. And co-requesting projects across the House and Senate is normal and completely legal.

Verdict: MISSING CONTEXT. The issue is framing, not legality. A lot of what he presents as "I secured" is shared with, and was often started by, the two Democratic senators he campaigns against.
