# FB Post: HR 9237 Rules Vote (DOCUMENTED PATTERN)

The Take Care of America's Veterans Act (HR 9237) has real wins in it: caregiver reforms, help for student veterans, more support for catastrophically disabled veterans. That part is true.

It also pays for its centerpiece by cutting future VA disability compensation for two of the most common service-connected conditions, sleep apnea and tinnitus. Existing ratings are protected, so no current veteran loses benefits, but new claims would be rated far lower. That is why the VFW, DAV, and IAVA all oppose the bill, and why House leadership pulled it from the floor.

Here is the part that is Langworthy's. He is not a cosponsor, and he is not on the Veterans' Affairs Committee. But he is on the Rules Committee, which decides what amendments the full House may vote on. On June 23:

→ He voted NO on the amendment that would have passed the popular Major Richard Star Act on its own, without the disability cuts (using other funds as the offset). It was kept off the floor.

→ He voted YES to report the closed rule that barred all amendments.

So there was a clean, recorded chance to deliver the veterans' benefit everyone praises without the cut the veterans' groups oppose. On the committee that controls the floor, he voted to keep that option off the table.

To be fair: closed rules and party-line committee votes are routine, and these were procedural votes about whether the House may vote, not on the ideas themselves.
