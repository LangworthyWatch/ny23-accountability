# FB Post: Buffalo Fireworks / Somali Flag (MISLEADING)

> Note: opening line uses the softened, motive-neutral framing (recommended for cross-aisle
> shareability and to match the site's no-speculation standard). If you prefer a sharper edge,
> swap the second sentence for: "Connecting two unrelated events so one seems to explain the
> other is an old move, and it only works if you don't check the timeline."

Wanting fireworks for America's 250th is a common desire. So when Congressman Langworthy pauses his press tour to post that Buffalo "canceled" its July 4 show while raising a Somali flag the same week, the "it was a choice about what they value" framing lands. But it works by welding together two unrelated events, one of which had nothing to do with the other.

Here's what the city actually said:

→ The mayor gave a specific safety reason: a vendor and sponsor (Russell Salvatore) were lined up, but no downtown site could clear a safe fireworks fallout zone near City Hall. A flag goes on a pole that's already there. Those aren't the same logistical problem.

→ The flag-raising wasn't a city stunt. It's an annual event run by the nonprofit Heal International, going back 4+ years. The same poles have flown Ukraine, Ireland, Greece, Puerto Rico, and more.

→ Nothing was "canceled." Buffalo hasn't held a city July 4 fireworks show since 2015. The mayor was trying to add one back downtown, and it fell through.

→ America 250 fireworks are still on: Buffalo and Erie County are holding them Aug. 2 at Canalside, launched from a barge on the river.

You can think the city should have tried harder and still see that "nothing to do with logistics" isn't what the record shows. The choice was never fireworks vs. no fireworks. It was an added downtown show vs. the barge show already booked for August.
