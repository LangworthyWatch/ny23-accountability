1,037 days. That's how long between Rep. Langworthy's two announcements of the exact same $1 million federal grant for Chautauqua County children's mental health services.

→ Sept. 13, 2023: "I'm pleased to announce this significant grant to provide needed resources for our community's mental health services."
→ July 16, 2026 (1,037 days later): "Every child deserves access to the mental health care... this $1 million investment will help ensure children... receive timely, comprehensive care."

Same SAMHSA program. Same $1,000,000. Same Chautauqua County Department of Mental Hygiene. Nearly the same sentence.

To be fair to him: both times, his own wording was modest. He said "announce" and "investment," not "secured" or "delivered." This isn't a false claim of credit.

What it is: a member of Congress does not pick the winner of this grant, SAMHSA does, and re-presenting the same or renewed federal funding as fresh news, twice, fits a pattern this site has documented before (see the Allegany water funding announced a third time).

The accountability turn: he voted Aye on H.R. 1 (Roll Call 190), roughly $900 billion cut from Medicaid, the program that funds most of the children's behavioral health care this one grant supplements.
