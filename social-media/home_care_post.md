# Facebook post: HCA-NYS meeting, the half he tells (July 2, 2026)
Companion to home_care_card.png and the fact-check.

---

Today Rep. Langworthy posted about meeting the Home Care Association of New York State: "We share a common goal: protect patients while being strong stewards of taxpayer dollars. By working collaboratively to root out fraud, waste, and abuse, we can strengthen home care."

Start with the credit, because it is real. In 2024 he signed the bipartisan New York delegation letter opposing Medicare home care cuts, and the association praised the delegation for it. On the Medicare side, "common goal" checks out, and we say so. (The association has taken no public position on the big Medicaid law, and a meeting is never an endorsement.)

Here is what the post leaves out.

→ The association's own published numbers describe a sector in erosion, not one padded with waste: 60 percent of NY home care and hospice agencies are losing money, 20 percent of agencies closed in the last five years, and more than 200,000 Medicaid patients could not get care.
→ His vote (Roll Call 190) cut the program home care actually runs on: roughly $900 billion out of Medicaid and SNAP over ten years, per CBO. Analysts are already tracking the law's home care effects.
→ The association he met has taken no public position on that law. The healthcare workers' union, 1199SEIU, has: "New York Republicans shamefully reneged on their promises to their constituents to protect Medicaid, instead voting for the largest cut in history."
→ And behind "root out abuse": the same vote carried a rider blocking the federal nursing home staffing rule for ten years. The administration then rescinded the rule entirely in December. And per ProPublica, the DOJ dropped about 23,000 pending criminal investigations last year, including one into a Virginia nursing home with a recent record of patient abuse.

The letter he signed in 2024 warned that home care cuts "will disrupt access to home health services." That logic was right. It just has not been applied to his own vote.

The post came one day after about 450,000 New Yorkers lost Essential Plan coverage under the same law. That did not come up either.
