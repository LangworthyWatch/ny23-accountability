We went through every post on Congressman Langworthy's official Facebook page, May through today. Then we counted, and we screenshotted everything.

→ Posts about beagles and animal testing: 30. Rallies, teasers, victory laps, "Together, we saved the Beagles."
→ Posts about the 450,000 New Yorkers who lost their Essential Plan health coverage on July 1: 0. That cliff is the direct result of H.R. 1, the law he voted for (Roll Call 190). About 26,000 of those people are in Western New York.
→ Posts about Springville's 80-bed nursing home entering closure, or Bradford Regional ending ER and inpatient care: 0.
→ Hospital posts: 10, every single one a funding announcement, a tour, or a LIVE stream.

The only closure he posted about all spring? Ridglan Farms. The beagle facility.

To be clear: nobody is against saving beagles. But beagle advocacy costs nothing. Explaining to 450,000 people why they lost their health coverage under your own vote costs something, and that is the post that never appears.

Every screenshot is archived on the fact-check, in case any of them disappear.
