# Social post — OBBBA / "largest tax cut" (Langworthy Watch)

Responds to Langworthy's July 4 anniversary post. Two variants; pick to match house voice. Numbers sourced in `analysis/obbba-deficit-burden-shift.md`.

---

## Variant A — with emoji (matches Langworthy's own bullet style)

Langworthy calls the July 4 tax law "the LARGEST TAX CUT in U.S. history."

It isn't — and the post leaves out who actually pays.

📉 It's about the 6th biggest tax cut since 1940 as a share of the economy (Tax Foundation). Reagan's 1981 cut was more than double the size.

💳 It adds $4.1 TRILLION to the national debt over ten years — $5.5 trillion if the parts set to expire in 2028 get extended, which is exactly what happened with the 2017 cuts.

⬆️ About 70% of the value goes to the top 10% of households (Penn Wharton). The middle fifth of families? Under $500 over a lifetime.

⏳ Those "no tax on tips / no tax on overtime / car-loan" perks he lists — they EXPIRE in 2028. The corporate tax breaks and the estate-tax cut for $15-million-plus estates? Permanent.

🩺 And part of the bill is paid for by cutting Medicaid and SNAP. So the lowest-income families lose health coverage and food aid now — and inherit the debt later.

Penn Wharton's bottom line: "all future generations are worse off."

That's not a tax cut for working families. It's a bill working families will be paying — twice.

*Sources: Congressional Budget Office, Joint Committee on Taxation, Penn Wharton Budget Model, Tax Foundation, Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget.*
