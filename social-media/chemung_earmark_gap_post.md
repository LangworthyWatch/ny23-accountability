POST ORDER: second of the two-post earmark set (the companion to "I Secured").

Nick Langworthy's earmarks give Chemung County $5.84 per resident, the lowest in NY-23, for one of its poorest and most populous counties. Its neighbors get more than $80.

We mapped every Community Project Funding earmark he directed (2024 to 2026) against Census poverty and population, dollars per resident:

→ Erie (his home area): about $85
→ Allegany: about $83
→ Cattaraugus (the district's poorest county): about $82
→ Chemung: $5.84

His one Chemung earmark went to a private college (Elmira College), not the municipal water, sewer, or public-safety projects he directed everywhere else. And the need was real and fundable: Senators Schumer and Gillibrand secured $1 million for a Chemung County wastewater consolidation. Langworthy did not.

To be fair, his earmarks broadly do track need across the rest of the district. Chemung is the exception.

Verdict: MISSING CONTEXT. We do not allege a deliberate snub. Earmarks require a local applicant, and the public record can't yet say whether Chemung's towns applied. But a poor, populous county getting $5.84 per resident deserves an answer.
