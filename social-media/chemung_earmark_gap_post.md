Where do Nick Langworthy's earmarks actually go? Mostly to the district's poorer rural counties, which is what you'd want to see. With one glaring exception.

We mapped every Community Project Funding earmark he directed (2024 to 2026) against Census poverty and population, dollars per resident:

→ Cattaraugus (the district's poorest county): about $82 per resident
→ Allegany: about $83
→ Erie (his home area): about $85
→ Chemung: $5.84

Chemung is populous, higher-poverty, and entirely inside NY-23. Yet his only earmark there went to a private college (Elmira College), not the kind of municipal water, sewer, or public-safety project he directed everywhere else.

And the need was real and fundable: Senators Schumer and Gillibrand secured $1 million for a Chemung County wastewater consolidation. Langworthy did not.

Verdict: MISSING CONTEXT. We do not allege a deliberate snub. Earmarks require a local applicant, and the public record can't yet say whether Chemung's towns applied. But a populous, higher-poverty county getting $5.84 per resident is a gap that deserves an answer.
