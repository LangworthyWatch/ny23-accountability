# FB Post: Committee Record on the Medicaid Cuts (DOCUMENTED PATTERN)

Every member of Congress wants a seat on the committees that matter. Langworthy holds two of the most powerful: Energy & Commerce, which writes health law, and Rules, which decides what amendments the full House is even allowed to vote on. His own website says he uses them "fighting to ensure that his constituents and our rural communities have a seat at the table on major legislation."

So here is the record on the biggest rural-health bill of his term, straight from the official committee reports, with his name next to every vote:

→ In the Energy & Commerce markup (May 13, 2025), he voted to advance the Medicaid cuts out of committee (work requirements, more frequent eligibility checks, a provider-tax freeze, new cost-sharing), voted down all 21 Democratic amendments to that section, and offered none of his own.

→ On the Rules Committee, four separate times, he voted to report "closed rules" that stopped the full House from ever voting on amendments to soften the cuts: striking the Medicaid cuts, making the ACA premium tax credits permanent (the ones tied to New York's Essential Plan cliff that cost about 450,000 New Yorkers their coverage on July 1), a $35 insulin cap, and reversing $313 billion in SNAP cuts.

→ Not one amendment of his own for a rural hospital, the Essential Plan, or anything specific to NY-23.

To be fair: closed rules and party-line committee votes are ordinary for a majority, and these Rules votes were procedural, about whether the floor could vote, not up-or-down on the ideas themselves. Grant all of that.

The point is narrower. He markets these seats as the district's leverage. The one rural-health win he points to (New York's $212 million) was added in the Senate, not by either of his committees. On the bill that mattered most to rural health, the seat at the table produced nothing of his own.
