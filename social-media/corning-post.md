## Finalized (house voice: no emoji, no em dash, arrow bullets)

Corning Incorporated is the largest private employer in NY-23, so its people backing their hometown congressman is not unusual. What the public record shows is a sequence worth seeing in one place.

→ While the 2025 reconciliation bill was being written, Corning spent more than $700,000 lobbying on it. Its own disclosures name the bill.

→ The law that passed kept the manufacturing tax credit Corning's solar plant relies on, and raised its semiconductor credit from 25% to 35%.

→ Langworthy voted yes (Roll Call 190) and called it "a generational win."

→ Then 62 Corning employees, from the CEO and CFO to engineers, gave him a combined $65,775. The largest gifts (CEO Wendell Weeks, the CFO, the CTO, the general counsel) arrived in September and October 2025, after the vote.

→ In spring 2026, his district office moved from Corning to Elmira, into the county where much of Corning's workforce lives.

We cannot prove a connection between the lobbying, the vote, and the money, and this does not claim one. It documents a sequence in the public record. You can decide what it means.

Sources: FEC bulk files, Senate lobbying disclosures, House Clerk (Roll Call 190), P.L. 119-21.
