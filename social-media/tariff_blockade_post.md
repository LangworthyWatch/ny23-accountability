# FB Post: Rules Committee Tariff-Vote Blockade (DOCUMENTED PATTERN)

When a president uses an emergency declaration to impose tariffs, federal law gives Congress a fast-track way to fight back: any member can force a floor vote to end them. It is one of the few real checks the House has.

Langworthy sits on the Rules Committee, which controls what reaches the floor. Twice in 2025, that committee attached a line to unrelated rules declaring that, for the rest of the year, each day "shall not constitute a calendar day" for that law's clock. No clock, no forced vote. A quiet off-switch.

The record, with his name on it:

→ March 2025 (H.Res.211): he voted to keep the language blocking a vote to end the Canada, Mexico, and China tariffs, and voted to send the rule to the floor.

→ April 2025 (H.Res.313): he did it again for the global "Liberation Day" tariffs.

The result: the House did not get its guaranteed vote to end the Canada tariffs until February 11, 2026, about a year later. When it finally came, Langworthy voted no again. Nine days after that, the Supreme Court ruled the tariffs illegal.

His own district was paying the bill. The Bush Industries plant in Jamestown closed in 2026, its filing naming tariffs among the causes, and local manufacturers depend on imported metal.

To be fair: both parties have used this procedural move to protect a president of their own party, and these were votes about whether the House may vote, not on the tariffs themselves. But on tariffs his own district's employers blamed for closing, he twice voted to keep the House from voting to end them.
