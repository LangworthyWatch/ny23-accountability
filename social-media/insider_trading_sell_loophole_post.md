"Members shouldn't run from a briefing to their broker," Langworthy says in his new video, touting his fight to "ban congressional stock trading."

His own bill's sponsor was asked point blank, on the record: can members still sell as much stock as they want? Rep. Bryan Steil's answer: "accurate but misleading."

Here's what H.R. 7008 (Stop Insider Trading Act), the bill Langworthy cosponsors, actually does:

→ Bans buying new individual stock.
→ Does not ban selling. Members can sell as much as they want, with 7 days' notice.
→ Crypto, private-placement stock, industry mutual funds, and bonds: all untouched.
→ The same bill carries Voter ID provisions stricter than Steil's own home state of Wisconsin, unmentioned in Langworthy's post. AOC called the pairing "a trick."

Rep. Neguse pressed the sponsor further: a Republican senator sold millions in stock 7 to 9 days before COVID hit in earnest. Under this bill, that stays legal, with notice.

Verdict: MISLEADING. Langworthy really did cosponsor a genuine reform bill. But its own sponsor has already conceded, on the record, that selling on a tip, exactly what his video says members shouldn't do, is the one thing the bill still allows.
