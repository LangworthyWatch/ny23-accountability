On June 8, Border Patrol apprehended 15 people in Marilla, in Erie County. According to CBP, all 15 were placed in removal proceedings. Those are the documented facts, and they're not in dispute.

When Rep. Langworthy shared the story, he added a claim: "While Democrats yet again voted to defund them, we are ensuring our law enforcement has the resources they need."

That claim is checkable. Here's the timeline:

→ April 30: DHS funding became law (P.L. 119-86) — the Senate passed it by voice vote
→ June 8: the Marilla operation — agents and a CBP helicopter, paid for by that already-enacted funding
→ June 9: the vote he's referring to — S. 2, a $70 BILLION supplemental on top of existing funding, which passed 214-212 with every Democrat opposed
→ June 10: the bill was signed, and the post went up hours later

Democrats voted against the new $70 billion while demanding conditions — judicial warrants, body cameras, limits on masks — after the Minneapolis shootings. You can agree or disagree with those conditions. But voting against an increase is not removing existing funding, and the operation he cites is itself evidence the funding was there.
