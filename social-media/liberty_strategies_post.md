# Facebook post: Liberty Strategies, the two firms, and the checkable ledger (July 2, 2026)
Companion to liberty_strategies_card.png and the fact-check.

---

Before Congress, Nick Langworthy ran his own political consulting firm, Liberty Opinion Research. State election records show its payments stop after 2018, and in July 2019 a state GOP spokeswoman said he was closing it as he became state party chair. That same January, a similarly named firm, Liberty Strategies LLC, was formed in Tonawanda. His congressional disclosures list his wife as the person earning income from it.

To be clear: they are different firms with different principals, and nobody is alleging wrongdoing. What we are saying is narrower. The system does not let you assess it.

→ His federal filing must name the source of spouse income, but not the amount. Every year, the amount reads "N/A."
→ Her state filing is the short form. It does not ask for a client list.
→ The firm's documented clients paid it $103,604 through 2022 (FEC records: Jacobs for Congress $71,035, Tom Reed PAC $17,569, Singletary 4 Congress $15,000).
→ Since 2022: not one payment to the firm appears in any public database, while it keeps appearing as household income on his filings.

Now compare stocks. At his June 25 tele-town hall, Langworthy said: "I don't trade stocks, I've never traded stocks since I've been in public office." We checked, because for stock trades the rules let anyone check: no transaction reports ever filed, one 401(k) rollover into the federal savings plan, index funds. His claim is true, and we say so.

That is the whole point. Where the rules make the record checkable, he checks out. Where they don't, no one can check anything, including him. Someone is presumably paying Liberty Strategies today. The public has no way to know who.

We sent his office six questions about the firm on June 24 and asked for a reply by July 1. We received nothing.
