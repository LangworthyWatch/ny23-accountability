# Facebook post: Liberty Strategies disclosure gap (July 2, 2026)
Companion to liberty_strategies_card.png and the fact-check.

---

Members of Congress file financial disclosures every year so you can see the income flowing into their household. That is a good rule. Here is what it actually shows, using one line from Rep. Langworthy's own filings.

His disclosures for 2022, 2024, and 2025 list spouse income from a political consulting firm called Liberty Strategies LLC. The amount listed: "N/A." House rules require naming the source, not the number.

Through 2022, you could still follow the firm's work, because its clients were campaigns that file their own reports:

→ $103,604 paid to Liberty Strategies by three NY Republican committees, 2019 to 2022 (FEC records, 39 itemized payments): Jacobs for Congress $71,035, Tom Reed PAC $17,569, Singletary 4 Congress $15,000.

Then the trail stops:

→ Since 2022, not one payment to the firm appears in the FEC or NY State election databases.
→ Yet the firm reappears as an income source on his 2024 and 2025 filings.
→ And in 2023 it vanishes from his filing entirely. Under the rules, that could mean income under $1,000 that year, or an omission. The record cannot tell you which.

To be clear: a spouse having a career is normal, political consulting is legal, and he disclosed the source exactly as the rules require. Nobody is alleging wrongdoing. The point is what the rules let stay invisible: the amount, the clients, and what the firm does now.

So we asked. On June 24 we sent his office six specific questions through its official contact form and requested a reply by July 1.

We received nothing.
