# FB Post: Northern Border Security Enhancement and Review Act (MISSING CONTEXT)

VERDICT: MISSING CONTEXT. A border bill branded as "Security and Enforcement" that adds no agents, no funding, and no new enforcement authority.

Securing the northern border is a real issue, and Rep. Langworthy is right that it deserves attention. He is promoting his "Northern Border Security and Enforcement Act," saying it "will ensure we are protected on all fronts."

Here is the context worth knowing before you share it:

→ The bill's real name is the Northern Border Security ENHANCEMENT AND REVIEW Act (H.R. 5517), and that name is the more accurate one. Every provision is a reporting or oversight step: it directs DHS to produce a Northern Border Threat Analysis, update its written strategy within 90 days, give Congress classified briefings, and build new performance metrics.

→ What it does NOT do: it adds no Border Patrol agents, appropriates no money, and creates no new enforcement authority. It is a study-and-measure bill, not a boots-on-the-border bill.

→ The "government watchdog" in the graphic is the GAO. Its late-June 2026 testimony (drawing on a February 2026 report) found the northern border's core problem is that Border Patrol staffing there FELL about 6% from 2019 to 2024, and that CBP still lacks measures to gauge its own effectiveness (a gap the GAO first flagged in 2019).

To be fair: H.R. 5517 answers one of the GAO's three recommendations, the one about performance metrics for Air and Marine Operations. That is a legitimate response to the watchdog. The other two, better metrics for Border Patrol and a plan to fix the specialist staffing gap, get nothing in the bill, and neither does the 6% drop in Border Patrol agents the same watchdog documented. It has also cleared committee (28 to 2 on June 24), but has not passed the House or become law.

Support border security? So do we. The point is narrower: a bill that orders reports and metrics is worth having, but it is not the same as the "protected on all fronts" enforcement the post implies.
