We fact-check Rep. Langworthy's claims. This one mostly holds up, and that belongs on the record too.

His posts: he "helped secure $60.5 million in @fema funds to rebuild Jasper-Troupsburg schools," and on WHAM 1180 said students "can learn in a safe, modern school, and families won't have to worry about the next flood."

What the record shows:

→ The award is real: $60,493,661 through FEMA's Public Assistance Program, 90% of a $67.2 million project for a campus flooded by Tropical Storm Fred (2021) and again by Debby (2024), mid-repair
→ The advocacy is documented: the district's superintendent thanked Langworthy by name for "steadfast support and advocacy throughout this process"
→ "Won't have to worry about the next flood" largely checks out: this isn't a same-site rebuild. The award includes $5M+ in hazard mitigation to relocate the school OUT of the floodplain to a significantly lower-risk site. Credit where due.
→ "Helped secure" is careful phrasing, more modest than Sen. Schumer's announcement of the identical award: "SCHUMER SECURES $60 MILLION"

Two pieces of context, not knocks: once FEMA approves a project, the money flows by statute. No member of Congress directs a specific award. And the superintendent credited both offices; neither announcement mentions the other.

The honest open question is about what comes next, not this award: a month before it, the President's FEMA Review Council proposed replacing the Public Assistance Program (the 90%-cost-share mechanism that funded this relocation) with fixed block grants to states. And in April 2025, FEMA canceled BRIC, its main PRE-disaster mitigation program ($5B+ since 2020), the one that helps other flood-prone communities reduce risk before the next loss, not after. Langworthy cosponsored the bipartisan FEMA Act, which points the other way; his position on the Council's plan is, so far, unstated.
