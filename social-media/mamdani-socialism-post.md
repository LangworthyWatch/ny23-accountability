## Finalized (house voice: no emoji, no em dash, arrow bullets)

Rep. Langworthy calls NYC Mayor Zohran Mamdani a "socialist" and a "communist." One label is fair. The other isn't, and the contrast is the story.

→ "Socialist" is his own word. Mamdani is a self-described democratic socialist and a DSA member. Fair game.

→ "Communist" is false. PolitiFact and Al Jazeera both rate it false. Communism means state ownership of the economy and the end of private property. Mamdani's program is public options inside a market: a freeze on already rent-regulated units, a pilot of five city-backed grocery stores operating alongside private supermarkets, universal childcare, fare-free buses. Public groceries are not new or radical. Think military commissaries, or state-run liquor stores in red states and blue ones alike.

→ Here is the part worth sitting with. The same idea Langworthy calls "communism" when it helps working people (government shaping who keeps what) he voted to make permanent when it flows up. In the tax law he championed and brands "Working Families," the breaks for tipped and overtime workers expire in 2028. The breaks for businesses, and the estate-tax cut for estates over $15 million, are permanent.

Public help for working people gets called "communism." Permanent tax breaks for capital get called "the free market." The honest debate was never whether government shapes the distribution. It is which direction it flows, and whose gains we agree to call by the same name.

Sources: PolitiFact, Al Jazeera, Congressional Budget Office, Joint Committee on Taxation.
