## Facebook

"You spoke. I listened."

That's how Rep. Langworthy described responding to animal-welfare advocates who asked him to look into Marshall Farms. Within days he visited in person and released video.

Here is who else has been speaking:

→ Constituents asking for an in-person town hall. He has not held one in over three years.
→ Constituents who write his office. The record is a stack of form letters, including one reply that arrived six days after the bill it was about had already become law.
→ A district carrying eight hospitals at risk of closure (the most in New York), mass layoffs in Chautauqua County, and a nursing home closing in Springville. No posts.

And the two breeding facilities he has spent weeks on? Ridglan Farms is in Wisconsin. Marshall Farms is in North Rose, Wayne County, which sits in NY-24, Rep. Tenney's district. Neither is in NY-23.

Caring about animals is good, and we are glad he went. The question is just this: what does a NY-23 constituent have to do to get the same "You spoke. I listened"?
