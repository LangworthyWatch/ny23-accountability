He says "I secured." About half of it, he shares with the two senators he campaigns against.

Of Nick Langworthy's roughly $32 million in enacted earmarks (2024 to 2026), about 54% are co-requested with Democratic Sens. Chuck Schumer and Kirsten Gillibrand, the senators he runs against.

Projects he shares with them:
→ West Seneca water, Portville water, Hornell water
→ North Chautauqua Lake sewer, Town of Friendship water
→ On several, the senators requested the project first, back in 2022

To be fair: about $14.9 million is genuinely his own, led by $4.2 million for the Erie County Sheriff's air unit. Co-requesting a project across the House and Senate is normal, and it helps the project get funded.

The point is not that any of it is improper. It is the word "I." The bipartisan, House-and-Senate reality goes missing when he says he secured it alone.

Verdict: MISSING CONTEXT.
