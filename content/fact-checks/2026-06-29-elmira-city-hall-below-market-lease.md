## Why This Matters for NY-23

A House member's district office is leased with federal money (the Members' Representational Allowance) and is supposed to be rented at fair market value — a rule the House lease form makes both landlord and member certify in writing. In early 2026, the City of Elmira proposed to lease Rep. Langworthy office space inside City Hall at a rate that, on the documents, sits about a third below the standard commercial rate that Chemung County applied for comparable space a few blocks away. A constituent who reviewed both contracts raised the point publicly. This page checks her claim against the two primary documents.

The key context, established up front: **the City Hall lease was never signed.** Langworthy withdrew before the council vote, and the office he actually opened — in the county-owned Hazlett Building — is leased at the commercial rate. So this documents a *proposed* below-market arrangement that did not happen, not a benefit that was consummated.

## The Claim

**Source:** Facebook video, constituent, June 2026

> As summarized from the video (paraphrase): the constituent says she reviewed the Elmira City Hall office contract and that the city's rate was too low — below market — noting that the county building Langworthy moved into instead charges roughly $850/month for about 510 square feet, even though the county building carries the added cost of guards and metal-detector ("wand") screening.

---

## The Two Leases (from primary documents)

Both are the standard **U.S. House of Representatives "District Office Lease" form (119th Congress)**, which requires the landlord to complete an amenities checklist and requires both parties to certify the rent is at fair market value.

| | Elmira City Hall (proposed) | Chemung County / Hazlett Bldg (executed) |
|---|---|---|
| Size | 400 sq ft | 510.63 sq ft |
| Rent | $450/month ($5,400/yr) | $855/month ($10,260/yr) |
| **Rent per sq ft/yr** | **$13.50** | **$20.09** |
| Term | Feb 3, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 | 1 year (effective ~June 1, 2026) |
| Paid by | House CAO (federal MRA) | House CAO (federal MRA) |
| Status | **Withdrawn before vote (Jan 12, 2026)** | **Approved 15–0 (May 11, 2026)** |
| Source | [Elmira City Council packet, Jan 12, 2026, Res. 2026-6](https://cityofelmira.granicus.com/AgendaViewer.php?view_id=1&event_id=221) | [Chemung Co. Legislature, May 11, 2026, Res. 26-218](https://chemungcony.portal.civicclerk.com/event/2339/files) |

The county's own internal justification for Resolution 26-218 states the figure plainly:

> "**Standard commercial rate being applied amounts to approximately $855/month, which includes utilities.** The congressional office has a standardized lease agreement."

So the county characterizes its $855 as the *commercial* rate. The City Hall figure is about a third below it on a per-square-foot basis.

---

## The amenities don't explain the gap — they widen it

A cheaper rate could be justified if City Hall offered far less. The opposite is true. City Hall's House-form checklist bundled **more** included services than the county's, for **less** per square foot:

| Included service (House lease checklist, Section A) | City Hall ($13.50/sq ft) | County ($20.09/sq ft) |
|---|---|---|
| High-speed internet | Yes (Empire) | Yes (Spectrum, upgrade pending) |
| Interior CAT-5e wiring | Yes | — |
| Lockable network space | Yes | Yes |
| Telephone service | Yes | Yes |
| Assigned parking | 2 spaces | Yes |
| Utilities | Yes | Yes (gas, electric, water) |
| Janitorial | Yes (as needed) | — |
| Trash removal | Yes (weekly) | Yes (weekly) |
| Carpet cleaning | Yes (as needed) | — |
| Window treatments | Yes | — |
| After-hours building access | Yes | — |
| On-site building manager | Yes | Yes |

**In plain language:** Elmira City Hall offered to include janitorial service, carpet cleaning, an extra parking space, window treatments, and after-hours access that the county lease does not — and still charged about $6.60 less per square foot per year. At the county's commercial rate, that 400-square-foot City Hall office would have cost roughly **$670/month** instead of $450 — about **$2,600 a year** below the local government benchmark. The constituent's "three guards and a wand" observation points the same direction: the county building delivers *more* (security screening) and charges *more*; the security overhead does not explain why the City Hall offer was lower.

## Context

- **It was never executed.** Langworthy withdrew from the City Hall proposal on January 12, 2026, before the council vote and before the House Office of Administrative Counsel — which reviews every district-office lease for fair-market-value compliance before signature — ever received it. Local outlets reported his stated reason as the City Hall space not meeting the full operational needs of a congressional district office. No money changed hands.
- **The discount was the city's offer, on the record.** The $450 figure came from a communication by Elmira City Manager P. Michael Collins to the council; the resolution (Res. 2026-6) authorized "the Mayor … to sign the Lease." There is no public record of Langworthy's office negotiating the figure down.
- **Who would have been subsidizing whom.** Because the rent is paid by the federal MRA, a below-market City Hall rate would mean *Elmira taxpayers* forgoing revenue to host a federal office — and the member's office receiving space below market. The House lease form's fair-market-value certification (Section B, ¶26: "The Lease … is entered into at fair market value as the result of a bona fide, arms-length, marketplace transaction") exists precisely to prevent a member from taking below-market space.
- **A documented relationship between the parties.** The official who would have signed the City Hall lease for the city, Mayor Daniel J. Mandell Jr. (R-Elmira), is a documented Langworthy donor ($250; FEC individual-contribution records). See the related entry below. This is a matter of public record; it is not evidence of any agreement about the lease.

## What is documented — and what is not

**Documented:**
- The proposed City Hall rate ($13.50/sq ft/yr, full-service) was about 33% below the rate Chemung County labeled the "standard commercial rate" ($20.09/sq ft/yr) for comparable first-floor government office space in the same city, weeks apart, for the same tenant.
- City Hall's lease bundled more included services than the county's, making the per-square-foot gap a conservative measure of the discount.
- Both leases are House District Office Lease forms carrying the mandatory fair-market-value certification.
- The City Hall lease was withdrawn before execution; the executed county lease is at the commercial rate.

**Not documented:**
- That Langworthy's office sought or negotiated the below-market figure (the record shows the city proposing it).
- Any connection between the mayor's donation and the lease terms.
- Any reason for the below-market figure (it could reflect the city's view of older space, a goodwill posture, or simple under-pricing). Public records do not resolve why.

## Questions This Raises

1. Would the proposed $450/month rate have satisfied the House fair-market-value certification, given the county's own "standard commercial rate" of $855/month for comparable space?
2. Who set the $450 figure on the city's side, and against what comparison?
3. Did the House Office of Administrative Counsel ever review the City Hall proposal before it was withdrawn?
