## Why This Matters for NY-23

Langworthy has repeatedly framed the One Big Beautiful Bill Act as putting "more money in your pocket" for working families. The Child Tax Credit did increase by $200 per child — that's real. But during the same period, his party imposed tariffs that cost the average household $1,300–$1,745/year, cut federal programs the district depends on, and launched a war that has cost taxpayers $51 billion and counting. This series runs the full tab.

**District baseline:** ~310,000 occupied households, median household income $70,756 (below national median), high car ownership (avg. 2/household), 47,592 manufacturing workers, significant agricultural counties.

## Post 1 — The Framing

**Headline:** "More money in your pocket." Here's what we found when we ran the numbers.

Rep. Langworthy keeps saying the Working Families Tax Cut is putting more money in your pocket. The Child Tax Credit went up $200 per child. For a family with two kids, that's $400/year.

That's real. We're not disputing it.

But here's what else happened while he was promoting that number:

→ Tariffs added $1,300–$1,745 to the average household's annual costs — actual Treasury collection data, not a projection
→ DOGE eliminated programs that specifically served this district — rural healthcare, manufacturing support, infrastructure funding
→ Operation Epic Fury has cost taxpayers $51 billion and counting at $1 billion per day — with gas prices spiking from Strait of Hormuz disruption
→ The same law that raised the CTC also cut Medicaid and SNAP by hundreds of billions

**The math for a median NY-23 family with two kids:**
→ CTC benefit: +$400/year
→ Tariff cost: -$1,300 to -$1,745/year
→ Net before war costs or DOGE cuts: **-$700 to -$1,345/year**

That's not "more money in your pocket." That's a net loss.

Over the next week, we'll break down each cost — with sources, dollar figures, and what it means for this district specifically.

All data sourced from: Senate Joint Economic Committee, Tax Foundation, Yale Budget Lab, Pentagon congressional briefings, CSIS, Congressional Budget Office, Brookings Institution.

## Post 2 — Tariffs

**Headline:** The Child Tax Credit went up $400. Tariffs took $1,745. Here's the math.

Langworthy promoted the $200/child CTC increase as a win for working families. For a family with two kids, that's $400/year.

Here's what he didn't mention:

→ The Joint Economic Committee — using actual U.S. Treasury revenue data, not projections — found tariffs cost the average American household **$1,745** in the 12 months ending January 2026
→ The Tax Foundation estimates ongoing tariff costs at **$1,000–$1,300/household/year**
→ Yale Budget Lab found the bottom 10% of earners pay **0.8% of their after-tax income** in tariff costs — the burden is regressive

**For NY-23 specifically:**
→ Median income is below the national average — tariffs hit harder as a share of income
→ 47,592 manufacturing workers face tariff-driven supply chain disruption
→ Agricultural counties (Cattaraugus, Chautauqua, Allegany) face retaliatory tariffs on farm exports
→ District-wide tariff cost: **$403 million to $541 million per year** across 310,000 households

**The net math:**
→ CTC increase for family of two: +$400/year
→ Tariff cost (JEC, actual data): -$1,745/year
→ **Net: -$1,345/year** — before accounting for anything else

Langworthy voted for the tax cut. He also voted against every effort to roll back tariffs. Both of those are his record.

Sources: Senate Joint Economic Committee (Treasury data), Tax Foundation, Yale Budget Lab, DataUSA/Census Bureau (NY-23 demographics).

Full fact-check: langworthywatch.org/fact-checks/2026-04-17-obbba-working-families/

## Post 3 — DOGE

**Headline:** "Efficiency" sounds good until you see what they actually cut.

DOGE doesn't have a clean per-household number — and we won't pretend it does. What it does have is a list of named programs, with documented dollar amounts, that specifically served this district. Here's what's gone or threatened:

**AmeriCorps — $26 million+ cut statewide**
→ 3,600 members terminated in New York
→ Southern Tier rural healthcare programs specifically cited in the cuts
→ These weren't bureaucrats — they were people staffing clinics in communities that don't have enough doctors

**FuzeHub / Manufacturing Extension Partnership**
→ Federal funding threatened for the statewide NY MEP serving small manufacturers
→ NY-23 has 47,592 manufacturing workers — these programs help small shops compete
→ Sen. Schumer flagged the threat to these centers in April 2025

**Northern Border Regional Commission — proposed elimination**
→ $48 million for 78 Upstate NY projects — infrastructure, broadband, economic development
→ These are targeted investments in exactly the kind of communities NY-23 is made of

**Water infrastructure — $580 million proposed reduction**
→ Clean Water and Drinking Water Revolving Fund grants for New York State
→ NY-23's aging rural water systems depend on this funding

**USDA Rural Development**
→ Positions providing rural loans and grants to Upstate NY communities eliminated
→ Rural Energy for America Program grants frozen

This isn't "cutting waste." These are named programs with documented local impact. Langworthy has not publicly opposed any of these cuts.

Sources: Sen. Schumer press releases (May 2025, April 2025), Center for American Progress (June/August 2025), Spectrum News (March 2025).

More at langworthywatch.org

## Post 4 — Operation Epic Fury

**Headline:** $51 billion spent. $1 billion a day. And gas prices are spiking.

Operation Epic Fury — the U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran that began February 28 — has now cost American taxpayers more than **$51 billion** based on Pentagon's own figures to Congress.

Here's how the costs add up:

→ **Days 1–6:** Pentagon briefed Congress on $11.3 billion (excludes pre-strike buildup and munitions replacement)
→ **Days 7–12:** CSIS updated the total to $16.5 billion
→ **Day 7 onward:** Pentagon estimated $1 billion/day ongoing (per WSJ)
→ **As of mid-April (~Day 46):** Running total exceeds **$51 billion**
→ **Long-term:** Harvard's Linda Bilmes projects $1 trillion when you include munitions replacement, veteran benefits, and debt interest

**What that means per household:**
→ Direct costs to date: ~$392 per U.S. household
→ Penn Wharton 2-month scenario: $305–$725 per household
→ Long-term projection: $7,600+ per household over time

**The local angle — gas prices:**
→ The Strait of Hormuz carries 20% of the world's daily oil supply
→ AAA reported the fastest weekly gas price increase since Russia's 2022 Ukraine invasion following early Epic Fury strikes
→ NY-23 averages 2 cars per household with a 22-minute commute
→ Every dollar increase in gas hits this district harder than most

**What $51 billion would cover domestically:**
→ The entire Northern Border Regional Commission for 1,000+ years
→ AmeriCorps nationally for 125+ years
→ The proposed water infrastructure cuts for NY reversed 88 times over

Congress has not authorized this war. No AUMF for Iran exists. No declaration of war has been issued. Langworthy has not called for a vote.

Sources: Pentagon congressional briefings, CSIS, Penn Wharton Budget Model, Harvard Kennedy School (Prof. Bilmes), CNN, WSJ, AAA.

Full analysis: langworthywatch.org

## Post 5 — The Full Ledger

**Headline:** What Langworthy said you're getting vs. what the tab actually looks like.

Over the past week we've gone through the numbers one by one. Here's the full picture for a median NY-23 household.

**What Langworthy says you're getting:**
→ Child Tax Credit: +$200/child ($400 for family of two)
→ Child Care Credit: "up to $3,000/$6,000" (actually expense caps — real credit is 20–50% of that)
→ FSA increase: up to $2,500 more pre-tax (if your employer offers one; effective 2026)
→ Trump Account: $1,000 per newborn (locked until age 18; not automatic)

**What the running tab actually looks like:**
→ Tariffs: -$1,300 to -$1,745/year (JEC, actual Treasury data)
→ Iran War (direct costs to date): -$392/household and counting
→ DOGE: tens to hundreds of millions in lost services for the district — named programs, documented cuts
→ Same law that raised CTC: cut Medicaid (10 million projected to lose coverage) and SNAP ($186 billion reduction)

**The bottom line:**
→ OBBBA benefit for family of two: ~$400–$600/year
→ Tariff cost alone: -$1,300–$1,745/year
→ **Net: -$700 to -$1,345/year — before war costs or DOGE cuts**

Add the Iran war's per-household share and you're looking at **-$1,700 to -$2,470 per household** in near-term costs his party added.

**What "more money in your pocket" actually means in NY-23:**
→ District-wide tariff cost: $403M–$541M/year
→ District share of war costs to date: ~$122M
→ Programs cut: rural healthcare, manufacturing support, water infrastructure, broadband
→ Programs cut by the same law he promoted: Medicaid and SNAP

We built this analysis from the Joint Economic Committee, Tax Foundation, Yale Budget Lab, CBO, CSIS, Pentagon briefings, and Brookings Institution. Every number is sourced. Every source is linked.

## Methodology Notes

All per-household figures use national estimates applied to NY-23's household base (~310,000 occupied households). Where national figures exist from named institutional sources (JEC, Yale Budget Lab, CSIS, Penn Wharton), those are used directly. NY-23 specific program cuts are noted separately. All estimates are conservative (lower-bound of ranges used unless otherwise noted). Tariff, Iran war, and litigation costs are *in addition to* OBBBA tax changes, not a comparison against them.
