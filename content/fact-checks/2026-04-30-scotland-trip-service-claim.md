## Why this matters in NY-23

Members of Congress can take privately-sponsored trips if the House Ethics Committee approves them. That rule exists to allow genuine study travel. It requires sponsors to file detailed itineraries and justifications — creating a paper trail that is public record.

This fact-check compares Rep. Langworthy's April 30, 2026 Facebook post about selfless public service against the documented record of his March 28–April 4, 2026 trip to Scotland, Ireland, and England — a trip that coincided with a partial federal government shutdown.

---

## Statement

**Source:** Facebook Post  
**Posted by:** Congressman Nick Langworthy (verified account)  
**Date:** April 30, 2026

> "Service over self. Our Founding Fathers wanted citizen legislators, selfless servants, who put people and not themselves first. I came to serve, not cash in. That's why I support a ban on Congressional stock trading. Our duty is to serve people, not our personal portfolios."

The post was published 26 days after Langworthy returned from a week-long trip to Scotland, Ireland, and England.

## The Facts

### The Trip

House Ethics Committee pre-travel approval letter (March 26, 2026) and post-travel disclosure (#500033838, filed with the Clerk of the House) document the following:

**Dates:** March 28 – April 4, 2026  
**Destinations:** Scotland · Ireland · England  
**Sponsor:** Republican Main Street Partnership (RMSP)  
**Langworthy's stated justification:** "Serves on the Energy & Commerce Committee, attending to study the environment and environmental protection."

**Accommodation:** Old Course Hotel, Golf Resort & Spa, St Andrews, Scotland — one of the world's most exclusive golf destinations.

**Documented itinerary:**

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Mar 28–31 | St Andrews — "stakeholder presentations," Edinburgh Castle tour, Falkland Palace tour |
| Apr 1–3 | Dublin — Trinity College, Leinster House, Meta HQ visit, Salesforce visit, Jameson Distillery dinner |
| Apr 3–4 | London — Churchill War Rooms, Westminster Abbey, "Working Dinner," departure |

Multiple blocks of "Executive Time" appear throughout the itinerary.

### The Shutdown Context

The trip proceeded during a partial federal government shutdown in which the Department of Homeland Security lacked full appropriations. Federal workers were going unpaid during this period, according to contemporaneous reporting.

The trip was covered nationally by TMZ (April 1, 2026: "30 Members of Congress in Scotland on Taxpayers' Dime Amid Shutdown"), Political Wire ("30 Members of Congress on Scotland Junket"), and Semafor ("Lawmakers get TMZ treatment during DHS shutdown").

### The Sponsor: Republican Main Street Partnership

RMSP is a 501(c)(4) social welfare organization — legally required to disclose nothing about its donors. Its CEO earns approximately $406,000 per year. In prior cycles, the organization's affiliated super PAC has reportedly been substantially funded by labor union PACs (International Union of Operating Engineers: $500,000; United Brotherhood of Carpenters: $500,000; LIUNA: $325,000). These super PAC contribution figures would appear in FEC filings rather than the 501(c)(4)'s nonprofit return, and have not yet been matched to a specific FEC committee filing in this entry. Its corporate membership base is not publicly disclosed.

### The "Environment" Justification

Langworthy's filing states he attended to "study the environment and environmental protection." The documented itinerary contains no environmental content. Activities include a Scottish golf resort stay, castle and palace tours, visits to Meta and Salesforce corporate offices, a whiskey distillery dinner, and multiple unscheduled "Executive Time" blocks.
