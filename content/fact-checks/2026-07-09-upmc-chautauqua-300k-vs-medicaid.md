## Why this matters

Langworthy has been touting **$300,000 he is seeking for a radiology training lab
at UPMC Chautauqua** — the Jamestown hospital where he was born. It is a good
project. But $300,000 for a training lab sits against a vote he cast that pulls
far more than that out of the same hospital, every year.

## The Facts

**1. The $300,000 is not secured — it is "in the base text of a bill."** Like his
Olean police announcement, this is an **FY2027 Community Project Funding request**
(on his official disclosure) riding an appropriations bill that has not passed the
House floor, the Senate, or become law. (See the companion Olean entry.)

**2. UPMC Chautauqua is one of the district's most Medicaid-dependent, financially
fragile hospitals.** Per the Fiscal Policy Institute, roughly **37% of its
patients are on Medicaid**, and it runs a deeply negative operating margin
(about **-17%**). Its finances rise and fall on Medicaid reimbursement.
*(Re-verify these two figures against the FPI source before publishing.)*

**3. He voted for the Medicaid cuts.** Langworthy voted **Yes on H.R. 1** (the
"One Big Beautiful Bill Act"), which cut hundreds of billions from Medicaid
through work requirements, more frequent redeterminations, a provider-tax
moratorium, and cost-sharing — the exact revenue streams a hospital like UPMC
Chautauqua depends on. (See our companion entries on his rural-hospital and
Medicaid votes.)

**In plain language:** He is publicizing $300,000 he hopes to bring to a hospital
whose survival depends on Medicaid — after voting to cut the Medicaid that keeps
its doors open. The cut dwarfs the grant.

---

## Context

The training lab is a legitimate, useful project, and celebrating a local hospital
is normal. The problem is proportion and sequence: a not-yet-secured **$300,000**
photo-op does not offset a **vote** that reduces the Medicaid dollars UPMC
Chautauqua relies on by far more than that — a textbook example of the "help with
one hand what you cut with the other" pattern this project documents.
