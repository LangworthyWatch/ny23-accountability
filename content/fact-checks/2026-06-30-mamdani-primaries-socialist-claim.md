## Why This Matters

Langworthy shares a Newsmax chyron — "MAMDANI-BACKED CANDIDATES SWEEP HOUSE PRIMARIES / IN GOLDMAN AND ESPAILLAT" — and adds his own framing. Unusually for a partisan attack post, the checkable facts are correct. The distortion is in scale and inference: the wins were real but geographically narrow, and the labels stretch past what the results show.

---

## Statement

**Source:** Facebook Post, June 2026

> "No question Socialist Mayor Zohran Kwame Mamdani is the dominant leader of the Democrats. His candidates defeated Democrats in primaries across NYS. Radical progressives and liberal extremists are the new norm in their party."

---

## The Facts

| Claim | Verdict | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Mamdani-backed candidate beat Goldman (NY-10) | **TRUE** | Brad Lander won ~62–38 |
| Mamdani-backed candidate beat Espaillat (NY-13) | **TRUE** | Darializa Avila Chevalier won ~49.3–46 (≈86% counted when called) |
| Mamdani endorsed the challengers | **TRUE** | Lander, Avila Chevalier, and Valdez (NY-7) |
| "A sweep" of his endorsed picks | **TRUE** | 3 for 3 in the June 23, 2026 primary |
| "across NYS" (statewide) | **MISLEADING** | All three are New York City districts; there was no upstate sweep |
| "dominant leader of the Democrats" / "new norm" | **OPINION / SPIN** | Wins were confined to deep-blue NYC seats |

**Worth noting:** Only two of the three were incumbent ousters (Goldman, Espaillat). Valdez (NY-7) won an *open* seat (~57–34). Espaillat had establishment backing — Jeffries, Hochul, James, the Congressional Hispanic Caucus, the state AFL-CIO — and lost anyway.

---

## Context

- **"Across NYS" is the load-bearing exaggeration.** Three NYC primaries is not a statewide result. NY-23 (Langworthy's own district) and the rest of upstate held no such contests; the post implies a sweep that did not happen outside the city.
- **"Socialist" vs. "communist"-adjacent framing.** Mamdani is a self-described democratic socialist and DSA-aligned; "socialist" is defensible as a label he accepts. The post's broader "radical progressives and liberal extremists are the new norm" is opinion, not a measured finding — three safe-seat NYC primaries do not establish a party-wide "norm."
- **The accurate core.** The Newsmax chyron itself (Goldman and Espaillat lost to Mamdani-backed challengers) is accurate, and this entry does not dispute it.

**In plain language:** The election results in the post are real. The geography ("across NYS") and the sweeping inference ("dominant leader," "new norm") are where it overreaches.
